Pipeline palette — 3 directions

Same content, same layout. Three different color systems applied to the 8 closeout statuses, so you can compare how the bar reads at a glance and how individual chips feel in context.

Option A 4-bucket lifecycle

Group statuses by what they mean operationally: in motion (orange), waiting (amber), won (green, deepening as money lands), closed (gray). Down from 8 hues to 4, with shade as the within-bucket variation. The bar now reads as a health summary, not a rainbow.

Why this works: Invoiced / Completed / Paid all read as “green = good,” so a healthy pipeline literally looks green. Disputed is a saturated red-orange — the only thing on the bar that demands attention. Cancelled drops to gray because nothing's at stake anymore. Lead is a quiet tan, not gray, so it doesn't get confused with closed states.

Option B Monochrome + 2 alarms

The pipeline is mostly grayscale (warm grays on the cream bg, deepening as a job progresses). Only two states get a real color: Disputed in brand orange (action required) and Paid in green (the win). Everything else is information without urgency.

Why this works: Most jobs are routine. Coloring every status equally creates noise; coloring only the two states an operator should *react* to makes them pop. The trade-off is that the pipeline bar is less of an at-a-glance health view — you have to read it.

Option C Soft tints, matched to the cream

Same 4-bucket logic as A, but every color is desaturated and warmed up so it sits naturally on the #fff8f4 background. Reads as calm and editorial rather than dashboard-y. Dark text on pale fills keeps it accessible.
