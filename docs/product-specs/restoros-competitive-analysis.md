## 1. Market Overview

### Industry Size

- **62,582** damage restoration businesses in the US (IBISWorld, 2025), growing at 4.3% YoY
- US damage restoration services market: **$7.2 billion** (2025)
- Water damage restoration market projected at **$5.97B** in 2026, reaching **$8.97B by 2032** (CAGR 6.93%)
- IICRC: **49,000+ certified technicians**, **6,500+ certified firms** globally
- Major franchises: SERVPRO (2,370+), ServiceMaster Restore (800+), Paul Davis (300+)

### Software TAM

With 62,582 restoration businesses and average software spend of $300-800/month, the **restoration software TAM is ~$225M-$600M/year in the US**. The addressable market for a modern all-in-one platform targeting 50,000+ small/mid shops: **$180M-$360M/year**.

### Technology Trends

- AI and IoT in water damage assessment: **$3 billion emerging market**
- Mobile-first adoption accelerating -- field techs live on phones
- Insurance carriers increasingly demand digital documentation
- Legacy software (DASH, 20+ years old) creating switching opportunities
- magicplan announced AI-powered Capture Mode for 2025-2026 -- validates voice-first approach

The undisputed industry standard. **99% of carriers require Xactimate-formatted estimates.**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|

### 2.2 Encircle

| Attribute | Detail |

## 4. Unsolved Pain Points (RestorOS Opportunity)

1. **No tool generates Xactimate-ready line items from photos.** Scoping takes 2-4 hours of manual data entry. RestorOS AI Photo Scope is genuinely first-to-market.

2. **No true voice-guided scoping workflow.** Others have voice-to-text notes. Nobody has structured, guided voice workflow that produces scope data.

3. **Documentation-to-estimate handoff is broken.** Contractors use one tool for docs, manually re-enter into Xactimate. Universal pain.

4. **Small shops priced out.** A 2-person company pays $425-770/month across tools. RestorOS can consolidate at $149/mo.

5. **Legacy UX creates training burden.** High technician turnover + complex legacy tools = expensive onboarding.

6. **Moisture logging disconnected from scoping.** Nobody ties readings, photos, equipment, and scope together.
